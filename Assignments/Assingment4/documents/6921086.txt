Title: Thomas Downs House


The Thomas Downs House is a historic home located just east of Charlestown, Indiana's town square.  It was built about 1809. and is a two-story, four bay, Federal style brick dwelling. It has a gable roof, sits on a stone foundation, and has a one-story rear ell.  Thomas Downs was a politician from Charlestown that was Clark County's first county treasurer.  He would later serve as an Indiana Territory legislator. Note: This includes  and Accompanying photographs.Kramer, Carl. This Place We Call Home (Indiana University Press, 2007) p. 39) It is owned by the Clark's Grant Historical Society, who has a museum there but offers tours by reservation only.

It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1984.
